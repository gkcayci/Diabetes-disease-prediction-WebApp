Diabetes is a chronic disease and there is no cure. However, keeping the blood sugar level within normal limits is important in minimizing the effects of the disease. For this, patient education, control of the patient's own blood sugar, proper nutrition and adequate exercise are of great importance in reducing the risk of complications in the long term.

Diabetes patients should have a routine check-up every 3 months. HbA1c tests are performed during these quarterly controls. Many different tests such as heart, kidney, eye and neurological tests are performed once a year. Therefore, it is very important to monitor diabetes patients regularly.

Diabetes is diagnosed in the internal medicine department or you can go to the endocrinology and metabolic diseases department. This department is a branch of internal medicine department. Pediatric patients are followed in the endochronic department.
